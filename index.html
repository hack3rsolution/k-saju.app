五行 · The Five Elements

Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water —
the quiet architecture of how we feel.

오행

Each of us carries a unique elemental signature, shaped by the moment we were born. Some elements run strong; others quiet. When they flow in harmony, we feel grounded.

When out of balance, we feel friction — the stress, the fatigue, the restlessness. Friction is information. Ohaeng helps you read it, gently.

木
Wood
Growth · Vitality
The energy of springtime — flexible, upward, reaching. Strong Wood brings vision and momentum; tense Wood brings restlessness.
火
Fire
Warmth · Expression
The energy of summer — radiant, expressive, joyful. Strong Fire brings connection; depleted Fire brings burnout.
土
Earth
Grounding · Care
The energy of late summer — steady, nurturing, present. Strong Earth brings stability; heavy Earth brings stagnation.
金
Metal
Clarity · Refinement
The energy of autumn — clear, precise, discerning. Strong Metal brings focus; brittle Metal brings rigidity.
水
Water
Depth · Stillness
The energy of winter — reflective, deep, adaptable. Strong Water brings wisdom; cold Water brings withdrawal.
